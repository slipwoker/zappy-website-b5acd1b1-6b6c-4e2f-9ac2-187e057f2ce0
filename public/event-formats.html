Frequently asked questions.

Planning a memorable event requires careful coordination and attention to detail. Below are answers to common questions about our event planning services, timelines, and delivery to help you prepare for your next corporate team-building experience or public treasure hunt.

How much lead time do you need to organize an event?

For most corporate events and team-building experiences, we recommend booking at least 3-4 weeks in advance to ensure optimal planning and venue coordination. However, we can accommodate shorter timelines for smaller groups or standard formats. Public treasure hunts typically follow a seasonal schedule announced 6-8 weeks ahead.

What types of venues work best for your events?

Our event formats are designed to be flexible and adaptable to various settings. We've successfully delivered experiences in urban parks, corporate campuses, hotel conference centers, historic districts, and private estates. The key requirement is adequate space for team movement and challenge stations, along with basic amenities like restrooms and shelter options.

Can you accommodate different group sizes?

Absolutely. Our event management services scale from intimate groups of 15-20 participants up to large corporate gatherings of 200+ attendees. We adjust team sizes, challenge complexity, and staffing levels to ensure every participant has an engaging experience regardless of total headcount. Smaller groups enjoy more personalized attention, while larger events benefit from competitive multi-team dynamics.

Do you offer both indoor and outdoor event options?

Yes, we design experiences for both indoor and outdoor environments. Outdoor treasure hunts take full advantage of natural landscapes and urban exploration, while indoor formats work perfectly for conference venues, museums, or corporate office spaces. We also offer hybrid models that blend indoor briefings with outdoor challenges, providing flexibility for weather contingencies.

What does Treasure Race provide on event day?

Our full-service event planning includes all materials, equipment, and staffing needed for a seamless experience. This encompasses challenge props, clue packets, team identification materials, scoring systems, safety briefings, on-site coordinators, and post-event wrap-up. Clients typically only need to arrange the venue, catering if desired, and participant transportation. We handle everything else from setup through teardown.
